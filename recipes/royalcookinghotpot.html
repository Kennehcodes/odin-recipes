Korean Royal Cooking: Mushroom Hotpot

This recipe was given to me in Korean at the Hansol Culinary Academy in Jongno South Korea. I made a vegetarian version in class and will share how I did that here.

Ingredients Needed

For the the Hotpot

  • King Oyster Mushroom: half of one
  • Shiitake Mushrooms: two
  • Oyster Mushrooms: fourth of a pack
  • Mushrooms: fourth of a container
  • White Cabbage: two leaves
  • Carrot: 1/6th of one
  • Minari: two stalks
  • Onion: one fourth
  • Crown Daisy: one stalk
  • Tofu: 1/8th of a block

For the Broth

  • Daikon Radish: 1/10th of one
  • Onion:1/8th of one
  • Leeks Stem: 2cm of a stalk
  • Leek Leaves: one stalk
  • Korean Soy Sauce for Soup

For the Dipping Sauce

  • Soy Sauce: 2 big spoons full
  • Sugar: 1 big spoon full
  • Vinegar: 1 big spoon full
  • Broth (from the Hotpot): 4 big spoons full
  • Cracked/Ground Seasame Seeds: one small spoon full
  • Brown Mustard or Wasabi: add to taste

How to Make the Hotpot

  1. First soak the crown daisy in water.
  2. Take the radish, onions, leeks and cut them up and way you'd like and boil them with five cups of water.
  3. Tear the enoki mushrooms, oyster mushrooms into bit sized portions for the hotpot.
  4. Chop the King Oyster mushroom, shiitake, cabbage, carrot, minari and onion into pieces around 5-6cm in length.
  5. Cut the tofu to a 1cm thickness.
  6. When the broth finishes prepare the dipping sauce. Just combine all the ingredients together and put into small bowls to dip the vegetables and tofu in.
  7. When the broth is fragant and ready after 30 minutes to 3 hours of boiling/simmerring, you can add the ingredients for the hotpot, boil them and eat them from the pot. Feel free to dip the vegetables in the dipping sauce that you made.
