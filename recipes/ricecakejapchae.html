Korean Royal Cooking: Stir Fried Burdock and Rice Cake

I made this dish at CJ The Kitchen in Seoul as a part of a vegetarian cooking class in 2018. This dish was pretty special to make and I learned that the term Japchae refers to anything cut long and thin and mixed. This is a different kind of japchae than you might be expecting but it is very delicious so give it a try!

Ingredients

For the dish

  • Burdock Root: 120g
  • Rice Cake: 150g
  • Green Bell Pepper: 1/2
  • Perilla Oil: 1 large tablespoon
  • Sunflower Oil: 1/2 large tablespoon

For the Sauce

  • Thick Soy Sauce: 1 large tablespoon
  • Korean Style Soy Sauce: 1 large tablespoon
  • Grain Syrup: 2 large tablespoons
  • Refined Rice Wine: 1 large tablespoon
  • Perilla Oil: 1 large tablespoon

For the garnish

  • Pine Nut Powder: 1/2 small tablespoon

Directions

  1. Peel the skin off the burdock root and slice them into thin, long strips. Place into cold vinegar bath to prevent browning.
  2. Cut the rice cakes into quarters, lengthwise. Be careful not to cut your fingers.
  3. Cut the green bell peppers into long, thin slices.
  4. Strain the burdock, pat dry and stir fry it with perilla and sunflower oil for over four minutes.
  5. Make the sauce by mixing both the soy sauce types, then adding the grain syrup, cooking wine, perilla oil and add the peppers last.
  6. After the burdock is done being fried, add the rice cake, sauce, and stir fry it all.
  7. After the rice cake has cooked, add the green bell peppers and stir fry quickly, finally adding perilla oil at the end.
  8. Plate and garnish with Pine nuts.
