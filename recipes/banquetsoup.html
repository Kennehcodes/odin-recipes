Korean Temple Cuisine: Banquet Noodle Soup

I made this dish at a Korean temple cooking class in Seoul and thought that the flavors were so robust and lovely for a vegetarian diet. This recipe was given to me by Korea Temple Food and I hope you enjoy it.

Ingredients

For soup

  • Thin Wheat Flour Noodle: enough for four servings
  • Napa Cabbage Kimchi: 100g
  • Shiitake Mushrooms: two
  • Zucchini: 1
  • Carrot: 1/4th
  • Roasted Seaweed Laver: two sheets
  • Perilla Oil
  • Homemade Soy Sauce
  • Vegetable Broth: 8 cups

For dipping sauce

  • Homemade Soy Sauce: 2 tbsp
  • Cheongyang Chili Peppers: 2
  • Green Peppers: 2
  • Ground Perilla Seed: 1 tbsp

How to Make the Soup

  1. Make the seasoning sauce first. Yess water out of the kimchi leaves and chop them into small strips.
  2. Afterwards roast the seaweed paper briefly and cut into thin strips using a scissors.
  3. Cut the shiitake mushrooms into thin strips. Season it with soy sauce and sesame oil and stir fry it.
  4. Slice the zucchini into thin strips and sprinkle with salt. Add some oil to a frying pan and stir fry until lightly cooked.
  5. Cut the carrot into small strips and pan fry it lightly, you can add a little bit of water to help it steam.
  6. Pour water into a pot with the noodles and let them boil.
  7. When the water boils add a half cup of cold water after about a minute. After it boils again, wait a minute and add another 1/2 cup of cold water.
  8. Strain the noodles and add cold water to them after the noodles come to a final boil.
  9. Heat vegetable broth add seasoning sauce to taste.
  10. Portion out noodles into bowls, arrange toppings on noodles and pour on the broth. Add the seaweed to the top and serve with seasoning sauce.
